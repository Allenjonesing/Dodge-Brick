Living Room Pirates v50 movement baseline - do not regress

Why v50 mattered:
- The physical ship/player must never move, rotate, bob, or drift in VR.
- The player remains at the room/Guardian origin for comfort.
- The game simulates travel by moving through virtual ocean coordinates.

Authoritative model:
- WaterOneGrid3x3.virtualShipOceanPosition is the virtual ship location in ocean space.
- Steering changes the virtual heading/travel direction.
- Sail percent and anchor state control travel speed.
- Rendered position for ocean-space objects is: oceanCoordinate - virtualShipOceanPosition, rotated by the ocean frame.

Debris rule:
- Debris owns a fixed ocean coordinate.
- Debris is never parented to the ship layout.
- Debris snaps vertically to the Water1 surface, but its X/Z are resolved from ocean coordinates.
- If debris looks glued to the ship, this rule has been broken.

Wake rule:
- Wake ripples are temporary ocean-space debris marks.
- They spawn near the ship edge/stern at the current ocean coordinate.
- They should stop spawning when anchor is down or speed is zero.
- They should fade/despawn, not be a fake UI trail attached to the ship.

Tile/wave rule:
- Water1 tiles are visual pages of the same ocean.
- Tiles recycle/render around virtualShipOceanPosition.
- Wave coordinate offset must advance with virtualShipOceanPosition so crests/troughs treadmill under the ship.
- Do not add a second independent wave scrolling direction.

Stationary dinghy rule:
- For a stationary/no-roomscale boundary, all hand controls must be reachable from the center.
- Mast/sail may extend above/outside boundary, cannon barrels may point outside, but wheel knobs, sail rope/cleat, anchor capstan, cannon breech/fuse, ammo pile, hammer, and leaks must remain reachable inside the rails.
